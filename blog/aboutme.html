Who is Frouk?

Hello! I'm Frouk, a passionate game modder and Software Engineer from Ukraine.
My journey began in 2019 when I started modding offline games using Cheat Engine. Over time, I expanded my skills to include Unity Engine modding also using Cheat Engine with mono features.
In my 9th grade, I took my time to research and learn C/C++ as well as Lua, x86/x64 Assembly and reverse engineering techinques.
Currently I'm focused on this website, various game mods and researching further into game and software development.

First game that I have modded was "Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas" back in 2019. It sparked my interest in game modding and development. Why GTA San Andreas? Since I didn't had powerful PC to handle games that were released that year
I decided to start with something that my PC could handle. Over time I moved to more complex games and engines.
Had an upgrade in 2023 which allowed me to explore more modern games and engines like Unity.

Over the years I tried modding various games including but not limited to: GTA San Andreas, Among Us, NFS Carbon, NFS Most Wanted, NFS ProStreet, Muck, Infinitode 2, Metal Gear Rising: Revengeance and more.
Each game presented its own challenges and learning opportunities, helping me to grow as a modder and developer.

Metal Gear Rising: Revengeance holds a special place in my heart as it was the first AAA game I modded. The experience was both challenging and rewarding, pushing me to deepen my understanding of game mechanics and modding techniques.
I've created several mods and tools for MGR, including a plugin framework, ImGui integration template, and a Mod Loader.
The list is included but not limited to: Photo Mod, Free Camera, Ini Tweaker, Mod Menu, Time Stop, Debug Menu(not for public) and more.

I finished my school in 2025 to later then focus on preparing for university and furthering my knowledge in Software Engineering and Game Development.
I'm excited to continue my journey in the world of game modding and development, constantly learning and pushing the boundaries of what's possible.

Feel free to explore my projects and blog posts to learn more about my work and experiences in the modding community!
